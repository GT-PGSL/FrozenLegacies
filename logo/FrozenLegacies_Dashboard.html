Selection Rationale (updated post-pivot)

ASTRA is now the primary tool. With the ratio calibration null, all quantitative power analysis comes from A-scope data alone. ASTRA's user-guided grid calibration directly references the oscilloscope graticule, giving the most reliable absolute dB values. R₀ will be computed per-CBD from ASTRA outputs using the full radar equation.

TERRA is secondary (spatial continuity only). Single-pixel Z-scope brightness cannot serve as a power proxy (~7 dB dynamic range from film saturation). TERRA still provides along-track ice thickness and spatial coverage beyond A-scope's CBD range, but not power information.

URSA is excluded for calibration due to the systematic +8 dB offset in surface power. For the absolute R₀ workflow, this offset would propagate directly into the reflection coefficient.

R₀ Workflow (Neal 1977 / GeometricCorrection.pdf)

Step 1 — Geometry: h_air = (t_surface − t_Tx)/2 × c_air; h_ice = (t_bed − t_surface)/2 × c_ice; h_eff = h_air + h_ice/n_ice

Step 2 — Absolute power: C = MDS(−101 dBm) − NoiseFloor(−60 dB) = −41 dBm; P_R = bed_dB + C + A_att(40 dB)

Step 3 — Power ratio: 10 log₁₀(P_R/P_T) = P_R(dBm) − 60 dBm

Step 4 — Dielectric loss: L_diel = 2∫α(T(z))dz [Johari & Charette 60 MHz]

Step 5 — R₀: R₀(dB) = 10 log₁₀(P_R/P_T) − 20 log₁₀(λG/8πh_eff) + L_diel   [specular: 20 log₁₀, not 40]

Single-Pixel vs. Windowed Brightness Extraction
Moot — the entire Z-scope brightness approach failed. Single-pixel dynamic range (~7 dB) is too compressed by film saturation for any extraction method to recover usable power information.
Resolved
Nonlinearity of Z-scope Brightness → Power
Moot — no relationship exists at any functional form (linear, log-linear, polynomial all tested). The differentiated display plus film saturation completely destroys the power information.
Question 1 (new)
Noise Floor Calibration for R₀
The ASTRA reference line represents the noise floor at −60 dB on the oscilloscope graticule. Does this correspond exactly to MDS (−101 dBm)? If the noise floor sits above MDS, the calibration constant C shifts. Need to verify against Neal 1977 Table 1.1.
Question 2 (new)
Geometric Correction: 20 log₁₀ vs 40 log₁₀
For specular reflectors (smooth bed), the geometric term uses 20 log₁₀ (P ∝ 1/R²). For rough/diffuse reflectors, 40 log₁₀ may be more appropriate (P ∝ 1/R⁴). The bed condition is unknown a priori — may need to compute both and use the correction as a diagnostic of specularity itself.
Question 3
A-scope Attenuator Setting Verification
The in-flight attenuator A_att = 40 dB is documented for the SPRI system (Neal 1977), but was it constant throughout F125? If the operator adjusted it mid-flight, we need to detect the transition in the data (look for step changes in surface_dB).
Question 4
Modern Comparison Grid Resolution
Inverted basal drag fields are gridded at 1–5 km, while SPRI data has ~300 m CBD spacing. Compare at native resolution (nearest-grid-cell) or smooth SPRI data to match?
